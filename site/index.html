Quick answer

What is InvoiceGen?

InvoiceGen is a local-first macOS invoice app and Rust CLI for freelancers, consultants, and small operators who want invoice data to stay on their Mac. The app manages clients, projects, payment details, invoice drafts, due dates, line items, discounts, tax rates, and PDF-ready exports without requiring an account or hosted backend. Its companion Rust CLI reads and writes the same local store format as the macOS app, so invoices can be listed, rendered, filtered, or automated from scripts. InvoiceGen is open source under the MIT License, free to download, and currently targets macOS 14 Sonoma or newer. It is best suited for solo operators who prefer a native desktop workflow, transparent local files, and simple automation over subscription invoicing platforms. For AI agents, the project publishes a repository skill at /SKILL.md with CLI commands, safety rules, sample workflows, and verification steps.

Features

Everything you need to invoice, local-first.

Local-first by design

Your client records, project details, and invoice history stay on your Mac. No signup walls, cloud servers, tracking SDKs, or subscription fees.

Draft & render invoices

Generate clean, client-ready formats with structured invoice numbers, due dates, line items, customizable tax rates, discounts, and payment terms.

Clients & projects

Store client contacts, currencies, and project rates once. Track billing context, project distribution, and yearly revenue without complex CRMs.

Status tracking

Keep workflow stages visible. Track draft, sent, overdue, paid, and void states to manage outstanding balances and client follow-ups instantly.
